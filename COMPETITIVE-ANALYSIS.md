# Competitive Analysis & Positioning — FounderMath

## Executive Summary

FounderMath competes in two categories:
1. **Cap Table Management:** Carta, Pulley, AngelList
2. **Educational Content/Tools:** Holloway Guides, online calculators

Our unique positioning: **Free, visual calculators for early-stage founders** — not a cap table management platform, not a consultancy.

---

## Competitive Landscape

### Carta

**What they are:** Full-featured cap table management platform for startups of all sizes.

**Target customer:** Funded startups (Series A+) with complex cap tables, 409A valuations, option plan administration.

**Key features:**
- Comprehensive cap table management
- 409A valuations
- Option plan administration
- Liquidity programs (secondary markets)
- Investor relations
- ASC 818 and tax compliance

**Pricing:** Expensive (typically thousands/month for full features, freemium for very early stage)

**FounderMath advantage:**
- Carta requires onboarding and account setup — we're instant, no signup
- Carta is overkill for pre-seed/seed founders — we focus on the math, not the administration
- Carta is for managing equity post-raise — we're for understanding equity before/during raising
- Carta's free tier is limited — our calculators are free forever

**Gap to exploit:** Carta's complexity. Many founders don't need full cap table management until Series A+. They just need to understand the math.

---

### Pulley

**What they are:** Simpler cap table management for early-stage startups.

**Target customer:** Seed-stage startups who need cap table tracking but find Carta too complex/expensive.

**Key features:**
- Clean, simple cap table UI
- Scenario modeling for fundraising
- Document generation
- Option pool management
- Integrations with payroll/legal

**Pricing:** Mid-tier (typically hundreds/month)

**FounderMath advantage:**
- Pulley is still a cap table platform with signup/onboarding — we're calculators with zero friction
- Pulley costs money immediately — we're free with optional Pro upgrade
- Pulley focuses on tracking — we focus on understanding (education first)

**Gap to exploit:** Pulley's pricing. Even $100-200/mo is significant for pre-revenue startups. We provide the educational tools to understand the math for free.

---

### Slicing Pie

**What they are:** Dynamic equity splitting methodology and calculator for early-stage co-founders.

**Target customer:** Founders at the very beginning trying to figure out how to split equity.

**Key features:**
- Time-based equity allocation
- Multiple contribution types (time, money, ideas, etc.)
- Formulaic approach to "fair" splits
- Calculator with various inputs

**Pricing:** Paid software, methodology consulting

**FounderMath advantage:**
- Slicing Pie is hyper-focused on one methodology — we have 6 calculators covering multiple aspects
- Slicing Pie charges for software — our dilution/vesting calculators are free
- Slicing Pie is methodology-specific — our calculators let founders model their own scenarios

**Gap to exploit:** Scope. Slicing Pie solves one problem. We solve the entire equity understanding problem.

---

### Holloway Guides

**What they are:** High-quality educational guides on startup topics (equity, cap tables, fundraising).

**Target customer:** Educated founders, investors, and operators who want deep understanding.

**Key features:**
- Comprehensive, well-written guides
- Expert authors and editors
- Curated resources and checklists
- Community discussions

**Pricing:** Paid guides (typically $100-200 per guide), plus consulting

**FounderMath advantage:**
- Holloway is read-only content — we're interactive calculators
- Holloway costs money for access — our blog and calculators are free
- Holloway is text-heavy — we visualize the math in real-time

**Gap to exploit:** Interactivity. Reading about dilution is abstract. Seeing your specific numbers change in real-time is immediate and actionable.

---

## Positioning Statement

**For early-stage startup founders** who are confused by equity math and fearful of making expensive mistakes, **FounderMath is a suite of free interactive calculators** that turns complex startup math into visualized, understandable results. **Unlike Carta or Holloway**, we require no signup, no payment, and provide instant answers to the questions keeping founders up at night.

## Feature Gaps We Can Exploit

### 1. Educational Focus

**Gap:** Most competitors are tools or content, not both.

**Opportunity:** FounderMath combines interactive calculators with educational blog content. Each calculator has a corresponding guide explaining the concepts.

### 2. Zero Friction

**Gap:** Carta, Pulley, Slicing Pie all require accounts, signups, and sometimes sales calls.

**Opportunity:** FounderMath is instant. No signup, no credit card, no friction. This lowers the barrier to understanding.

### 3. Visual-First Design

**Gap:** Most cap table software is data-first (spreadsheets with nicer UI). Educational content is text-first.

**Opportunity:** FounderMath is visual-first. Charts, graphs, and visualizations make complex math intuitive.

### 4. Pricing Accessibility

**Gap:** Even "simple" competitors cost $100-500/mo. Educational guides cost $100-200 each.

**Opportunity:** FounderMath is free forever for core calculators. $19/mo for Pro features is 10x cheaper than competitors.

## Competitive Weaknesses (to monitor)

### What we DON'T do (and that's okay):

1. **No cap table management:** We don't track ownership over time, issue shares, or handle compliance. That's Carta's domain.
2. **No legal documents:** We don't generate legal documents or offer legal advice. That's for lawyers.
3. **No 409A valuations:** We don't provide official valuations. That's for valuation firms.
4. **No investor relations:** We don't manage investor communications or liquidity.

**Positioning:** These aren't weaknesses—they're focus. We're not trying to be a cap table platform. We're the educational tool that helps founders BEFORE they need those platforms.

## Positioning for Different User Segments

### Pre-Seed Founders (0-5 employees)

**Message:** "You're fundraising. Investors are talking about dilution, SAFE notes, and option pools. You nod along, but do you really get it? FounderMath shows you exactly what happens to your equity—no spreadsheet required."

**Calculator focus:** Dilution, SAFE, Cap Table

### Seed Founders (5-20 employees)

**Message:** "You've raised money. Now you have employees to hire, vesting schedules to manage, and runway to track. FounderMath helps you understand the math so you can make informed decisions without asking your lawyer for every number."

**Calculator focus:** Vesting, Runway, Unit Economics

### Growth-Stage Founders (Series A+)

**Message:** "You probably have Carta or Pulley now. But do your employees understand their equity? Use FounderMath as an onboarding tool—let them see the math themselves."

**Calculator focus:** Dilution (for transparency), Vesting (for education)

### Prospective Founders

**Message:** "Thinking about starting a startup? Equity is the founder's currency. Before you issue your first share, understand the math. FounderMath shows you what equity dilution actually looks like."

**Calculator focus:** Dilution (founder scenarios), Vesting

### Early Employees

**Message:** "You were offered 0.5% equity. Is that good? What does it actually mean? FounderMath helps you understand your offer in real terms—no jargon, no confusion."

**Calculator focus:** Dilution (reverse calculator), Vesting (for their grants)

## Conclusion

FounderMath's positioning is clear:

**We're the educational layer that sits *before* the cap table management layer.**

Carta and Pulley manage your equity *after* you've made decisions. FounderMath helps you *make* those decisions in the first place.

This is a defensible position because:
1. Educational tools require less infrastructure than management platforms
2. We can iterate faster on calculators than cartas can on compliance features
3. Our zero-friction model is viral-friendly (easy to share)
4. We can expand to cover more educational calculators without changing our core model

The key is staying focused: **Math education, not equity management.**
