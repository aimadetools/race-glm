1/14
Startup equity compensation is confusing.

ISOs? NSOs? RSUs? Vesting? Cliff? 83(b)?

One wrong move can cost you tens of thousands in taxes.

Here's everything startup employees need to know about equity.

🧵

2/14
The main equity types:

• ISOs (Incentive Stock Options) — Tax-advantaged, capped at $100K/yr grant
• NSOs (Non-qualified Stock Options) — No cap, taxed as ordinary income
• RSUs (Restricted Stock Units) — No strike price, taxed on vest
• Restricted Stock — Actual ownership from day 1

3/14
ISO vs NSO — which is better?

ISOs:
• Tax-free until sale (if held 1 yr + 2 yrs)
• Subject to AMT
• Capped at $100K grant value/year
• Only for employees (not contractors)

NSOs:
• Taxed as income when exercised
• No AMT
• No grant cap
• For anyone (employees, advisors, contractors)

4/14
Vesting basics:

• Cliff: 1 year (you get nothing if you leave before)
• Total vesting: 4 years standard
• Schedule: Monthly or quarterly
• Acceleration: Single-trigger (acquisition) or double-trigger (acquisition + termination)

5/14
Example: You get 48,000 options vesting monthly over 4 years.

Leave after 18 months = 18 months vested = 18,000 options.
Leave after 6 months = before cliff = 0 options.

The cliff protects the company from early churn.

6/14
83(b) election — file within 30 days or lose big.

File 83(b) → Pay taxes NOW on FMV → Future growth = capital gains

Don't file → Pay taxes LATER at vest → Pay income tax on entire gain

83(b) is crucial for early-stage employees.

7/14
Tax timeline for ISOs:

Grant → No tax
Vest → No tax
Exercise → No regular tax, possible AMT
Sell (held <1 yr + 2 yrs) → Short-term capital gains
Sell (held >1 yr + 2 yrs) → Long-term capital gains (15-20%)

8/14
Tax timeline for NSOs:

Grant → No tax
Vest → No tax
Exercise → Ordinary income tax on gain (FMV - strike)
Sell → Capital gains tax on additional gain

9/14
Tax timeline for RSUs:

Grant → No tax
Vest → Ordinary income tax on FMV (withholding taken)
Sell → Capital gains tax on additional gain

RSUs are taxed at vesting regardless of whether you sell.

10/14
Strike price matters.

Strike price from your first startup: $0.50
Exit at $5/share = $4.50 gain per share

Strike price from later-stage startup: $4.00
Exit at $5/share = $1.00 gain per share

Early stage = more upside.

11/14
Common equity mistakes:

• Not understanding your strike price
• Filing 83(b) late (or not at all)
• Leaving before cliff
• Not modeling exit scenarios
• Not asking about option pool size
• Ignoring tax planning

12/14
How to evaluate your offer:

1. Grant size in options/shares
2. Strike price vs current valuation
3. Vesting schedule & cliff
4. Type of equity (ISO/NSO/RSU)
5. Company stage & exit probability
6. Your tax situation

13/14
Equity benchmarks by stage (senior engineer):

Seed ($0-10M raised): 0.25-0.75%
Series A ($10-50M raised): 0.1-0.4%
Series B+ ($50M+ raised): 0.05-0.2%

Later stage = lower % but potentially higher absolute value.
