1/12
Most startup employees accept their equity offer without negotiation.

They're leaving 20-50% more equity on the table.

Here's how to negotiate startup equity like a pro — benchmarks, tactics, and scripts.

🧵

2/12
Let's start with the math.

Seed-stage offer: 0.1% equity
At $100M exit: $100,000

Negotiate to 0.15% (+50%)
At $100M exit: $150,000

At $500M exit, that difference becomes $250,000.

Equity offers ARE negotiable. Founders budget for it.

3/12
Benchmarks by role & stage (Series A example):

• Founding Engineer: 0.5-1.5%
• Senior Engineer: 0.25-0.75%
• Mid-Level Engineer: 0.1-0.4%
• Head of Product: 0.5-1.5%
• Senior PM: 0.25-0.75%
• Head of Sales: 0.5-1.25%

Seed stage is 2-3x higher.

4/12
How to negotiate (script):

"I'm excited about this role. I researched equity benchmarks for senior engineers at Series A companies. The typical range is 0.25-0.75%. The current offer of 0.2% is below this range. Is there flexibility to get closer to 0.4%?"

5/12
Use the "Sandwich Method":

1. POSITIVE: "I'm genuinely excited about this role and the team."

2. COUNTER: [Your ask with data]

3. POSITIVE: "I really want to make this work and am eager to contribute."

6/12
If they can't give more equity, negotiate these instead:

• Better equity type (ISOs vs NSOs)
• Lower strike price
• Accelerated vesting on acquisition
• Longer exercise window (90 days → 1 year)
• Higher salary instead
• Signing bonus

7/12
Red flags in equity offers — walk away if you see:

• No 409A valuation
• Strike price above 409A
• Unreasonable vesting (cliff > 1yr, vesting > 4yr)
• Vague equity terms (won't tell you total shares)
• "We'll give you more equity later" (get it in writing)

8/12
Best leverage you have: Another offer.

"I have another offer for a similar role at [Competitor] for 0.5% equity. I'm more excited about your company and the team. Would you be open to matching the 0.5%?"

Alternatives drive better terms.

9/12
Time your ask.

Negotiate AFTER you have an offer, not before.

Once they've decided to hire you, they have skin in the game.

Good response: "Thank you! I'd like a day or two to review and discuss with family. When should I get back to you?"

10/12
The key data points you need before negotiating:

• Your current equity value (strike, vested/unvested)
• Company funding history & cap table
• Your leverage (skills, track record, alternatives)
• Modeled offer at different exit scenarios
