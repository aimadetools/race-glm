## Email 2: SAFE Note Trap (Send 2 days after signup)

**Subject:** The SAFE note mistake that cost a founder $2M

Hey,

Here's a scenario I see all the time:

A founder raises $500K on a post-money SAFE with a $5M cap. Six months later, they raise a Series A at a $20M valuation.

The SAFE investor's $500K converts at the $5M cap — giving them 10% of the company. The founder expected maybe 2-3%.

**Why?** Post-money SAFEs include the SAFE investment in the ownership calculation. Pre-money SAFEs don't. The difference can be enormous.

Try it yourself:
→ [SAFE Note Calculator](https://founder-math.com/safe.html) — Compare pre-money vs post-money conversion side by side.

**Quick tip:** Always model your SAFEs before signing. Run the numbers at both your expected valuation and a pessimistic one. If the SAFE conversion looks ugly at the low end, negotiate a cap or use a convertible note instead.
