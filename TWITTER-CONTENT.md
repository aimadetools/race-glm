### Thread 1: Startup Valuation Mistakes
**Post:** A thread on common valuation mistakes founders make

**Content:**
1. Mistake #1: Anchoring on a number you "deserve" — Most founders start with "I want $10M" without data. Use Berkus, Scorecard, or VC method instead.

2. Mistake #2: Ignoring dilution from SAFEs — If you raised on a $5M SAFE cap, your effective pre-money for Series A is lower than you think. Model it.

3. Mistake #3: Comparing to outliers — "Company X raised at $100M and we're similar" — unless you have the metrics to back it up.

4. Mistake #4: Valuing effort instead of results — "We spent 18 months building this" — Investors care about traction and customers.

5. Mistake #5: Not preparing for down rounds — Have a plan B. Investors lose confidence when you can't answer tough questions.

### Thread 2: Equity Dilution Myths
**Post:** Busting common myths about equity dilution

**Content:**
1. Myth #1: "Dilution means you're losing money" — Wrong. It's about giving up ownership to grow the pie. A smaller slice of a bigger pie can be worth more.

2. Myth #2: "I should avoid dilution at all costs" — Smart founders raise strategically. Every round is an opportunity to grow faster. The goal isn't to own 100% of nothing.

3. Myth #3: "Investors will dilute me unfairly" — Fair terms exist. Anti-dilution provisions, pre-money vs post-money clarity. Know your rights.

4. Myth #4: "I can negotiate my way out of dilution" — You can, but focus on the total deal, not just the percentage.

5. Myth #5: "Once I dilute, I can never recover" — Vesting protects you, not hurts you. 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff is standard for good reason.

### Thread 3: SAFE Note Tips
**Post:** Everything founders should know about SAFE notes

**Content:**
1. Choose the right SAFE type — Discount vs Cap vs MFN. When to use each based on your stage and investor relationship.

2. Understand conversion mechanics — Your SAFE converts at the next priced round. Know whether it's pre-money or post-money. The math matters.

3. Watch for valuation caps — Higher cap = lower effective pre-money when SAFE converts. Negotiate this number.

4. Know what happens in down rounds — Most SAFEs have no full ratchet. Lower valuation = bad outcome. Consider anti-dilution if concerned.

5. Keep track of your cap table — Every SAFE changes your future cap table. Update it immediately after signing.

**CTA:** Model your SAFE conversion and cap table changes with our calculators. foundermath.com/safe

### Thread 4: Vesting Explained
**Post:** Visual breakdown of how vesting works

**Content:**
1. What is a 1-year cliff? — You get 0 equity for your first year. After that, you vest monthly. It protects the company from early departures.

2. Single vs double trigger acceleration — Single trigger: acquisition vests remaining unvested equity. Double trigger: acquisition AND termination. Negotiate what you want.

3. Why 83(b) matters — If you don't file within 30 days of grant, your options are taxed as ordinary income when you exercise. Could mean thousands in extra taxes.

4. Early exercise window — Standard is 90 days after termination. Some companies offer longer. Know what you're getting into.

5. What happens if you leave before cliff? — You walk away with nothing. The company keeps the unvested equity. Harsh but legal reality.

**CTA:** Use our free Vesting Calculator to visualize your vesting schedule. foundermath.com/vesting

### Thread 5: Runway Planning
**Post:** How to think about runway strategically

**Content:**
1. The 6-month rule — Start fundraising when you have 6+ months of runway. Wait until 3 months and you're negotiating from weakness.

2. Growth matters — If revenue grows 10%/month, your effective runway increases. Ignoring growth = underestimating runway by months.

3. Variable burn — Expenses grow as you scale. Don't just use current burn. Project it out.

4. Buffer is your friend — Always have a 2-3 month cash buffer. Unexpected delays happen. Investors want to see you can execute.

5. Revenue projections matter — Investors will pressure-test your numbers. Have realistic growth assumptions and be able to defend them.

**CTA:** Calculate your runway with our free calculator. foundermath.com/runway

### Thread 6: Cap Table Best Practices
**Post:** What every founder should know about cap tables

**Content:**
1. Keep it up to date — Every grant, SAFE conversion, funding round changes your cap table. Update immediately.

2. Track fully-diluted vs outstanding — There's a difference. Investors look at fully-diluted (including option pool). Be clear which one you're using.

3. Option pool management — When to create, when to refresh, and how to size. Don't let pool run dry mid-hiring cycle.

4. Waterfall analysis — For exits, know the payout order. Participating preferred, liquidation preference, founder trigger. Model different scenarios.

5. Projections matter — Cap tables show your future structure at various exit valuations. Use this in fundraising conversations.

### Thread 7: Startup Offer Red Flag Scanner
**Post:** A thread on hidden red flags in startup offer letters

**Content:**
1. Most startup offers have hidden terms that silently wipe out 10-30% of your equity. Here's what to look for:

2. Red Flag #1: Double-trigger acceleration — You only vest if acquisition AND termination happen. Most startups only exit once. If you're fired before exit → $0. Negotiate single-trigger.

3. Red Flag #2: "At company discretion" exercise window — Standard is 90 days post-termination. If they can deny your exercise, your options become worthless. Never sign this.

4. Red Flag #3: No cliff provision — Sounds generous but removes leverage for both parties. Standard 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff exists for good reason.

5. Red Flag #4: Narrow-based anti-dilution — Only considers preferred shares. Broad-based includes all outstanding shares. Narrow-based silently dilutes employees more.

6. Red Flag #5: Missing 409A disclosure — You can't verify your strike price discount. Ask for last 409A, date, and next one planned.

### Thread 9: Founder Equity Score
**Post:** A thread on how to score your startup equity package

**Content:**
1. Is your startup equity offer good or bad? Most people can't tell. Here's a framework to score your equity (0-100):

2. Score component #1: Ownership percentage — 1%+ at seed = 20 points. 0.5%+ = 15 points. Below 0.5% = 10 points. Early employees should own meaningful equity.

3. Score component #2: Strike price discount — 80%+ discount to 409A = 20 points. 50-79% = 15 points. Below 50% = 5 points. You're getting equity for cheap — make sure it's actually cheap.

4. Score component #3: Vesting terms — Standard 4-year/1-year cliff = 15 points. Single-trigger acceleration = +5 points. Double-trigger = -5 points. Know what you're signing.

5. Score component #4: Exercise window — 90+ days = 15 points. Extended (7-10 years) = +5 points. "At discretion" = -15 points. Your options expire fast unless you negotiate.

6. Score component #5: Company quality — Funded by top tier = 10 points. Strong traction = +5 points. Pre-revenue pre-seed = 0 points. High risk needs high upside.

7. Total score breakdown: 80-100 = Excellent offer worth considering. 60-79 = Good with some tradeoffs. 40-59 = Negotiate or decline. Below 40 = Walk away.
