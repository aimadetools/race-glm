## Twitter Threads

### Twitter Thread: 50 Startup Equity Terms Explained

```
1/ 🧵 50 startup equity terms explained in one thread

Every founder has felt lost staring at a term sheet.

I built a free glossary at FounderMath, but here's the quick version:

#Startups #Equity #Founders

2/50
💵 ACQUISER
The company buying another in an M&A deal. If you're the acquirer, you're paying. If you're acquired, you're the target.

3/50
🎯 ACQUISITION HIRE
Someone hired as part of an acquisition. Often has a different vesting schedule than founders.

4/50
📉 DILUTION
Your ownership % decreases when the company issues more shares. Usually from fundraising or employee option pools.

Interactive dilution calculator: founder-math.com/equity-dilution

5/50
🛡️ ANTI-DILUTION
Protection that prevents investors from losing ownership % in future down-rounds. Full ratchet = brutal for founders.

6/50
🎓 BOARD OF DIRECTORS
Elected group that oversees the company. Investors typically get board seats as part of funding rounds.

7/50
🌉 BRIDGE ROUND
Funding between major rounds. Usually convertible debt or SAFE notes. "Bridge to nowhere" = bad sign.

8/50
💰 BURN RATE
How fast you spend cash each month. Burn too fast → runway runs out. Burn too slow → might not grow.

Runway calculator: founder-math.com/runway

9/50
💵 CAPPED NOTE
A convertible note or SAFE with a maximum valuation cap. Protects early investors from being diluted by high valuations.

10/50
📊 CAP TABLE
Spreadsheet showing who owns what in your company. Founders, investors, employees, and option pool.

Interactive cap table builder: founder-math.com/cap-table

11/50
🏁 CARRIED INTEREST
The profit share GP partners receive from their fund. Usually 20% of profits.

12/50
📉 CLIFF
Waiting period before equity starts vesting. Standard 1-year cliff. Leave before = 0% equity.

13/50
🎯 CLOSING
The moment the deal is done. Documents signed, money wired, equity allocated. Celebrate — then get back to work.

14/50
🤝 CO-FOUNDER
Someone who starts the company with you. Your equity split should be negotiated early and documented.

Co-founder split calculator: founder-math.com/cofounder-equity-split

15/50
⏰ COMMON STOCK
Regular shares employees and founders typically hold. Liquidation preference means preferred gets paid first.

16/50
💵 CONVERTIBLE NOTE
Debt that converts to equity in future financing. Has interest, maturity date, and usually a valuation cap.

SAFE note calculator: founder-math.com/safe-note

17/50
🔥 DEFAULT ALIVE
Paul Graham's framework: Can you survive indefinitely without raising? If no, you're "default dead."

18/50
📉 DOWN ROUND
Funding at a LOWER valuation than previous round. Signals problems. Dilution is painful. Investors get anti-dilution.

19/50
🎯 DUE DILIGENCE
Investors checking everything before they fund you. Financials, legal, IP, team. Takes weeks. Be prepared.

20/50
📊 DYNAMIC SPLIT
Co-founder equity that adjusts based on contribution over time. Fair but complex. Needs clear framework.

21/50
💵 EARNBACK
Investor requirement: founder equity vests backward from exit. "Earn back" full ownership only if company succeeds.

22/50
📉 EXIT
Selling the company or IPO. The moment founders get paid (if there's money left after liquidation preference).

23/50
🌉 EXIT MULTIPLE
What investors aim for. 3x return = good. 10x = unicorn territory. 100x = legend.

24/50
📊 83(b) ELECTION
IRS filing option to pay taxes on equity NOW instead of when it vests. Saves money if stock appreciates. 30-day deadline!

25/50
🎯 FAIR MARKET VALUE
What your shares are worth according to the IRS. 409A valuation sets this. Lower value = lower strike price for options.

26/50
🤝 FULL RATCHET
Most extreme anti-dilution. If you raise at a lower valuation, early investors get their ownership % fully restored. Brutal.

27/50
📉 FUNDING ROUND
Selling equity to investors for cash. Pre-seed → Seed → Series A → Series B → ... Exit.

28/50
💵 GRANT
Equity given to employees or founders. Usually subject to vesting. Vesting schedule terms are everything.

29/50
🌉 HORIZON
Timeframe investors look at. Seed investors see 5-7 years. Growth investors see 3-5 years.

30/50
💵 INSIDER ROUND
Funding from existing investors. Good signal they still believe. Downside: no new validation from fresh investors.

31/50
🎯 INVESTOR UPDATE
Monthly email to investors. Metrics, challenges, plans. Transparency builds trust. Ghosting destroys it.

32/50
📉 LIQUIDATION PREFERENCE
Investor guarantee: get their money back (with preferred terms) before anyone else in an exit. 1x is standard.

33/50
🌉 MATURITY DATE
When convertible debt MUST be repaid if not converted. Creates deadline pressure. Make sure runway covers it.

34/50
💵 NO-CLIFF
Immediate vesting starts from day one. Rare. Most companies want commitment first.

35/50
🎯 OPTION POOL
Shares set aside for future hires. Usually 10-20%. Expands before financing (dilutes founders). Expands after (dilutes investors).

36/50
💵 PRE-MONEY VALUATION
Company value BEFORE new funding. The number that actually matters for dilution.

37/50
📉 POST-MONEY VALUATION
Company value AFTER new funding. Pre-money + invested amount. Often the headline number.

Valuation calculator: founder-math.com/startup-valuation

38/50
🌉 PREFFERED STOCK
Investor shares with special rights. Liquidation preference, voting, information rights. Get paid before common.

39/50
🎯 PRO-RATA
Right to maintain ownership % in future rounds. Investors love it. Means you're giving them more equity in future.

40/50
💵 PURCHASE AGREEMENT
Legal document outlining investment terms. Part of closing package. Have your lawyer review every clause.

41/50
📉 ROUND PARTICIPATION
How much an investor puts in relative to the round. Lead investor = biggest check, sets terms.

42/50
🌉 ROUND SIZE
Total amount raised in a funding round. Bigger rounds = more dilution now, potentially more runway later.

43/50
🎯 SAFE
"Simple Agreement for Future Equity." YC's invention. Better than convertible notes. No interest, no maturity date.

44/50
💵 SAFE STACKING
Multiple SAFE notes converting in one round. Can silently kill founder ownership. Model before signing.

SAFE stacking calculator: founder-math.com/safe-waterfall

45/50
📉 SEED ROUND
First institutional funding. Usually $1M-$5M. Proves product-market fit to investors. Sets stage for Series A.

46/50
🌉 SERIES A
First major VC round. Usually $5M-$15M. Metrics-driven. Need traction, team, and clear path to scale.

47/50
🎯 SHARE CLASSIFICATION
Different types of shares with different rights. Common vs Preferred, voting vs non-voting.

48/50
💵 STRIKE PRICE
Price at which you can buy stock options. Lower = more upside. Set by 409A valuation.

49/50
📉 TERM SHEET
Non-binding outline of investment terms. The starting point for negotiations. Read every line carefully.

50/50
🌉 VESTING
Time-based ownership of equity. Standard: 4 years, 1-year cliff. Leave early = lose unvested portion.

Vesting calculator: founder-math.com/vesting-schedule

📊 That's 50 terms in 50 tweets.

Full glossary with definitions and calculators: founder-math.com/equity-glossary

Save this thread for your next equity conversation! 🎯
```
