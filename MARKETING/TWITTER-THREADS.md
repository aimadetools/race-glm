## Thread 2: SAFE Notes

1/7 Your SAFE note has a cap AND a discount.

Which one actually matters at conversion?

It's the LOWER valuation.

Example: You have a $5M cap and 20% discount. Company raises at $6M.

With cap: Conversion price = $5M / post-money
With discount: Conversion price = $6M × 0.8 / post-money

The discount wins you a better deal.

But wait — what if you have TWO SAFEs with different terms?

One has $5M cap, other has MFN.

The MFN SAFE gets the BEST conversion price from all other SAFEs.

So it benefits from the capped one but gets MFN protection too.

This stuff is confusing. I spent hours with spreadsheets.

## Thread 4: Unit Economics

1/8 What's your SaaS LTV:CAC ratio?

If you can't answer instantly, you're in trouble.

Because this is THE metric investors care about.

LTV:CAC = How much a customer is worth ÷ How much it costs to acquire them

Here's what healthy looks like by stage:

Pre-seed: 1.5:1 — you're finding product-market fit
Seed: 2.5:1 — showing early traction
Series A: 3:1 — sustainable unit economics
Series B+: 4:1 — efficient growth engine

Below 2:1? You're losing money on every customer.

Above 5:1? You're underinvesting in growth — could be growing faster.
